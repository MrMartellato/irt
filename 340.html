Arrival Procedures


LPV Approaches Approaches to LPV minima are categorized as approaches with vertical guidance (APV) approaches. These approaches are like LNAV/VNAV approaches in that they also provide lateral and vertical guidance on a stabilized glidepath, down to a specified decision altitude (DA). The difference is that LPV approaches are entirely dependent on Class 3 WAAS, for both the lateral and vertical guidance. This means that the altimeter errors associated with BARO-VNAV approaches are not applicable, and approach minima can be lower. The lowest DA that is currently available for LPV approaches is 200 feet above ground.


Aircraft must be equipped with avionics certified under TSO C145a/146a (Class 3 WAAS) to fly a GPS approach to LPV minima. This makes LPV approaches more accessible to general aviation as a multi-sensor FMS is not required.
